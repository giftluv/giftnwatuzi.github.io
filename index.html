About

Modern artificial intelligence systems have achieved remarkable progress across domains such as healthcare, cybersecurity, and intelligent infrastructure. However, deploying these systems in real-world environments introduces fundamental challenges related to data privacy, security, fairness, and trustworthiness. In many high-stakes applications, machine learning models must operate across distributed environments where sensitive data cannot be centralized, where adversarial threats are common, and where ethical and regulatory constraints demand transparent and accountable decision-making. Addressing these challenges requires new frameworks that combine advanced machine learning techniques with rigorous security, privacy, and reliability guarantees.

My research focuses on developing secure, trustworthy, and privacy-preserving artificial intelligence systems, with particular emphasis on federated learning, quantum-resilient cybersecurity, and explainable machine learning frameworks. I design intelligent systems that enable collaborative learning across distributed data sources while protecting sensitive information and ensuring robustness against adversarial threats. My work integrates concepts from machine learning, cryptography, distributed systems, and causal inference to build next-generation AI infrastructures that are both technically reliable and socially responsible.

My research can be broadly organized into the following research themes.

Secure and Privacy-Preserving Federated Learning

Federated learning enables multiple institutions to collaboratively train machine learning models without directly sharing raw data. While this paradigm significantly improves privacy, it also introduces new vulnerabilities, including model poisoning attacks, gradient leakage, and unreliable participant behavior. My research develops secure aggregation mechanisms and adaptive trust frameworks that strengthen the robustness of federated learning systems.

In this work, I investigate trust-weighted aggregation mechanisms, post-quantum cryptographic techniques, and privacy-preserving communication protocols that protect distributed learning environments. My proposed frameworks integrate homomorphic encryption and trust-aware client participation strategies to ensure that federated learning systems remain resilient to malicious participants while preserving model performance. These approaches aim to make collaborative machine learning viable for sensitive domains such as healthcare, where secure data sharing is essential.

Trustworthy and Explainable Artificial Intelligence

As machine learning systems increasingly influence critical decisions, ensuring fairness, transparency, and accountability becomes essential. My research explores methods for diagnosing and mitigating bias in AI systems using causal reasoning and explainability techniques.

Through my work on FairAudit, I develop frameworks that analyze how machine learning models behave across intersectional demographic groups and identify sources of unfair decision-making. By integrating causal analysis with model auditing techniques, this research provides actionable insights that help developers detect bias and implement corrective strategies. The broader goal is to enable machine learning systems that are not only accurate but also equitable and interpretable.

AI-Driven Cybersecurity and Intelligent Systems

Another core focus of my research is the application of artificial intelligence to cybersecurity, network monitoring, and intelligent infrastructure systems. Modern digital ecosystems generate massive volumes of network and sensor data that must be analyzed in real time to detect threats and anomalies.

My work develops deep learning-based intrusion detection and anomaly detection systems, including hybrid frameworks that combine classical machine learning with emerging quantum-inspired approaches. These systems aim to improve the detection of sophisticated cyber threats while maintaining scalability and operational efficiency. In parallel, I explore multimodal AI systems that integrate machine vision with IoT sensing, enabling intelligent monitoring platforms for complex environments such as healthcare systems, smart infrastructure, and autonomous monitoring applications.

Teaching and Mentorship

In addition to my research, I am deeply committed to teaching and mentoring the next generation of computer scientists. As a Graduate Teaching Assistant in the Department of Computer Science at Kent State University, I teach and support core undergraduate courses including Computer Science I (Programming and Problem Solving), Procedural Programming, Object-Oriented Programming, and Design and Analysis of Algorithms (CS-46101-020). Through these courses, I help students build strong foundations in computational thinking, algorithmic design, data structures, and software development principles.

My teaching approach emphasizes clarity, structure, and active engagement. I focus on breaking down complex concepts—such as recursion, algorithmic efficiency, asymptotic analysis, and algorithm design paradigms—into intuitive and practical components. I design and implement fair and consistent grading rubrics, develop assessments aligned with learning objectives, and provide detailed, constructive feedback to support continuous student improvement.

I actively mentor students both inside and outside the classroom by holding regular office hours, offering one-on-one guidance, and supporting diverse learning needs. I encourage students to think critically, write efficient code, and understand not just how algorithms work, but why they are designed the way they are. I also guide students in applying theoretical concepts to real-world problems, preparing them for both industry and research pathways.

Beyond instruction, I am committed to fostering an inclusive and supportive learning environment where students feel confident to ask questions, explore ideas, and grow intellectually. I integrate discussions on ethical computing, responsible AI, and real-world applications to help students connect foundational knowledge with broader societal impact.
